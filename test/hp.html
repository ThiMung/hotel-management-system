NMT Hotel History

Built in 1898, the red-brick building that now houses NMT Hotel began its life as a riverside warehouse along the East River, where sailors unloaded crates of coffee, spices, and textiles. For decades, it stood as a witness to change — from steamships to skyscrapers, from handwritten ledgers to electric lights.

In 2011, a young architect discovered this forgotten structure. Over two years of restoration, every window was mended by hand, every steel beam polished but not hidden — preserving its soul while welcoming a new chapter.
